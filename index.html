Narrative and Expository Writing


  1. Narrative vs. Expository Paragraphs:
    • Narrative paragraphs tell stories, like your exciting day at the park.
    • Expository paragraphs explain things, like how to make a peanut butter sandwich.
    Example:
    • Narrative: "Yesterday, I saw a huge rainbow after the rain. It was so colourful!"
    • Expository: "Rainbows appear when sunlight shines through water droplets in the air."

  2. Organising Information by Main Idea: It's like making a sandwich - the main idea is the filling, and the details are the bread!
    3. Example:
    Main idea:"Dogs make great pets."
    Details:
    • Dogs are friendly.
    • Dogs can learn tricks.
    • Dogs love to play with their owners.

  3. Putting Sentences in Order: Think of it like following a recipe - each step leads to the next!
    4. Example:
    How to brush your teeth:
    • Wet your toothbrush.
    • Put toothpaste on the brush.
    • Brush all your teeth carefully.
    • Rinse your mouth with water.
